Chapter One

From Crisis
to a Culinary Quest

The story of Mysore Raman Idli begins with an unexpected turn of events during the COVID-19 pandemic. Sandeep, a professional chef who specialized in continental grilling, lost his livelihood overnight. Devastated but determined to survive, he and his wife decided to start a pure vegetarian restaurant built on one radical principle: a kitchen with absolutely no freezers for storing leftover food.

"A kitchen with absolutely no freezers. Every single dish, served fresh."

Ironically, Sandeep was never a fan of idlis, he was a hardcore dosa lover. But his wife had a different idea. She suggested they explore the famous Thatte Idli (plate idli) from Bidadi, a town near Mysore. And so, seeking the perfect dish to anchor their new venture, they packed up and traveled to Mysore.

02

Chapter Two

The Search for
the Perfect Idli

Their journey initially met with disappointment. As they tasted idlis from various popular shops in Bidadi, Sandeep's chef instincts quickly detected something wrong, the makers were using baking soda and yeast, giving the idlis an artificial, appam-like flavour. He knew this wouldn't work.

They were on the verge of abandoning the idea entirely and heading home.

"Just as they were about to leave, they heard whispers about a woman making idlis unlike any other."

She didn't have a shop. She didn't advertise. She only supplied to homes. To taste her food, they had to extend their stay in Bangalore, waiting patiently for one chance.

03

Chapter Three

Meeting Seetha Patti
& a Sacred Promise

Seetha Patti

The woman behind the recipe

The maker was an elderly woman named Seetha Patti. After her husband, a brilliant idli maker, passed away years ago, she had taken over making his idlis simply to survive. When Sandeep and his wife tasted her idlis, they were blown away. It was the softest, most delicious idli they had ever eaten. Completely free of baking soda. Pure and honest.

Desperate and moved, they pleaded with her for the recipe, explaining their financial struggles, their tears visible. Seetha Patti listened. And then, without asking for a single rupee, she shared her complete secret.

The Oath on Camphor

"She made them take a solemn oath on camphor, karpooram, that they would name their new venture after her late husband, Raman."

And so, honouring both the place and the man, Mysore Raman Idli was born.

04

Chapter Four

Mastering
the Secret Recipe

Armed with Seetha Patti's exact measurements of urad dal and rice, they returned to Kochi, and failed. The batter wouldn't ferment properly. Unwilling to give up, they traveled back to sit beside her and watch her make the batter from the very beginning.

That's when Sandeep noticed it. She hand-ground the batter while constantly splashing cold water. And in that simple act lay the entire secret.

The Discovery

Friction Heat

Commercial grinders were heating the urad dal and cooking it prematurely, ruining the batter before it was even made. Cold water during grinding was the answer all along.

By modifying their grinding technique, using cold water to prevent friction heat, they finally replicated Seetha Patti's flawless, cloud-soft idlis. The breakthrough had arrived.

05

Chapter Five

The Struggle Behind
the 5-Hour Promise

Evening Service

Taking Seetha Patti's recipe to a commercial scale brought a new crisis. Because the batter has absolutely no preservatives, it loses its magic and fermentation quality after exactly 5 hours. For the first few months, morning customers were happy, but evening customers complained. The batter had lost its perfection.

"Refusing to compromise, Sandeep implemented a strict new rule: a completely fresh batch of batter is ground every single day at 12:00 PM, strictly for the evening service."

No refrigerating leftovers. No shortcuts. Two fresh batches, every single day. It was labor-intensive, but it was the only way to honour the promise.

06

Chapter Six

The Secret
of the Butter

The inspiration for the brand's famous Butter Idli came directly from Seetha Patti's humble home. Because she had no refrigerator, she would keep homemade butter in a vessel of water to keep it fresh, and serve a simple dollop of it alongside her idlis.

No chutney. No sambar. Just the idli and the butter. Because when the idli is truly pure, it doesn't need anything else.

Signature Dish

"The idli is so pure, it melts perfectly with the butter. Today, this unadulterated combination remains our best-selling dish, and the truest tribute to Seetha Patti's kitchen."

Our Commitment Today

A promise made
on camphor, kept every day.

Zero Additives

No baking soda. No yeast. 100% natural fermentation, just as Seetha Patti taught us.

5-Hour Freshness

Our batter has a strict 5-hour shelf life. Two fresh batches are ground daily, no exceptions.

No Freezers

We do not refrigerate leftover food. Everything served is made fresh that day. Always.

Raman's Legacy

Every idli we serve is a tribute to Raman, Seetha Patti, and the belief that the best food is made with honesty and love.

What started as a desperate search for survival turned into a passion that transformed a dosa-lover into an idli-enthusiast. If you visit our shop and say you aren't a fan of idlis, don't be surprised if Sandeep personally insists you try just one.

We promise it will change your mind.
